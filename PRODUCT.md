# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Late-night music seekers in introspective or contemplative moments who want to find songs that match their current emotional state. These users are tired of generic "top 100" playlists and keyword searches; they think in feelings, not genre names. They want discovery that feels personal and intentional, and they're often alone or with close friends when using the app.

## Product Purpose

MoodPlaylist generates personalized song playlists by translating emotional descriptions into music recommendations. The user describes how they're feeling in natural language, the app uses AI to interpret the mood and suggest artists and genres, then retrieves real Spotify tracks that match that emotional palette. Success means the user hears a song that genuinely resonates with their state of mind and discovers an artist they didn't know before.

## Positioning

MoodPlaylist bridges the gap between how people *feel* and what they want to *hear*—turning emotion into music discovery without forcing you into predefined playlists or genre silos.

## Brand Personality

The brand is **experimental, playful, and colorful**. This is not a serious, corporate tool; it's intimate and slightly whimsical. The interface should feel like a creative collaboration between the user's mood and AI-generated musical intuition. It should surprise and delight, not overwhelm. Minimal and intentional, never cluttered.

## Anti-references

Avoid chaotic layouts, disorder, or visual noise. Don't mimic Spotify's corporate dark aesthetic, SoundCloud's cramped grids, or algorithmic playlists that feel soulless. The interface should feel human-centered and calm, even when it's colorful.

## Design Principles

- **Emotion first**: Every interaction and color choice reflects the user's mood. The interface itself should be a sensory extension of their emotional state.
- **Discovery over catalog**: Design for finding one perfect song, not browsing a thousand results.
- **Clarity in playfulness**: Colors and animation should feel joyful, not chaotic. Intentional motion, never gratuitous.
- **Minimal friction**: The fastest path from "I'm feeling..." to "here's a song" wins.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA compliance minimum
- Full keyboard navigation; all interactive elements accessible via Tab/Enter
- Semantic HTML and ARIA labels for screen reader users
- Reduced-motion support: all animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`
- Color contrast minimum 4.5:1 for body text; interactive elements at least 3:1
- No color-only information cues; mood/status must be conveyed via text, icon, or position as well
